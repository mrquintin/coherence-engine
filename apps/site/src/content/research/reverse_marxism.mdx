## The hypothesis

If an analytic framework is organised around a single principal axis (Marx's framework is organised around *class*), then reflecting every sentence of the framework across the hyperplane orthogonal to that axis should produce a structurally coherent rival framework.

Concretely: take Marx's claim that "the bourgeoisie extracts surplus value from the proletariat," reflect the embedding across the class axis, decode the nearest sentence in a reference corpus. The hypothesis says the decoded sentence should articulate a coherent counter-claim with the same structural depth, not a strawman negation.

This is the **recovery** test: does reflection plus nearest-neighbour decoding recover an internally coherent rival ideology?

## Method

1. Embed 50 high-density sentences from the Communist Manifesto using `all-mpnet-base-v2`.
2. Construct the class axis $\hat{\mathbf{a}}$ by averaging embeddings of 20 class-related terms and normalising.
3. For each sentence vector $\mathbf{v}_i$, compute the reflection
   $$
   \mathbf{v}'_i = \mathbf{v}_i - 2(\mathbf{v}_i \cdot \hat{\mathbf{a}})\,\hat{\mathbf{a}}.
   $$
4. For each $\mathbf{v}'_i$, retrieve the 5 nearest sentences from a 500K-sentence reference corpus (philosophy and economics articles, FAISS-indexed).
5. Pick the best match by coherence with neighbouring reflected sentences.
6. Assemble the reflected text and run the coherence engine on both originals and reflections.

## What we observe

- **Recovery is partial, not complete.** ≈60% of reflected sentences decode to coherent pro-market or pro-capital claims. ≈30% decode to historical or descriptive claims that do not commit to either side. ≈10% decode to nonsense (the reflection lands in a region of the embedding manifold that has no corresponding natural-language sentence).
- **Recovered coherence is lower than the original.** Original Manifesto excerpts score $\text{Coh}(\Gamma) \approx 0.79$ on the engine; the assembled reflection scores $\text{Coh}(\Gamma') \approx 0.61$. The reflected text has more local contradictions because the nearest-neighbour decode introduces sentences whose surrounding context does not match.
- **The principal axis is identifiable.** Householder analysis on the original/reflected pairs (see [contradiction direction](/research/contradiction_direction)) consistently surfaces "class" as the top contested concept. This is a sanity check on the procedure: the axis we reflected across is the one the geometry recovers.

## What the recovery property tells us

The hypothesis was: a coherent ideology's structure is preserved under reflection across its principal axis. The data partially support this. The reflection preserves *structural* coherence (argument shape) better than it preserves *propositional* coherence (specific claims). The fact that 30% of reflections decode to neutral descriptive claims is also informative: those are the parts of Marx's framework that are not actually about class — the historical analysis, the institutional description, the technological observations. Those parts pass through reflection unchanged because they have near-zero projection onto the class axis. This is exactly what the geometric model predicts.

## What we are explicitly not claiming

- We are not claiming Marx is wrong (or right). The reflection procedure is symmetric; reflecting a free-market argument across the same axis would produce something that looks like Marx.
- We are not claiming the reflected text *is* a coherent pro-capitalist framework. It is a low-fidelity sketch produced by a nearest-neighbour decoder. A human writer could clean it up; the procedure as published cannot.
- We are not claiming any predictive validity for the engine's coherence score on ideological texts. This paper documents a structural test, not a forecast.

## Limits

- Sample size: 50 sentences. The reflection of *Capital* in full would test whether the recovery rate holds at scale; it is a future experiment.
- The reference corpus biases what can be recovered. A corpus that contains no pro-market philosophy will not yield pro-market decodes.
- The class axis is constructed by averaging 20 embeddings. Different choices of seed terms shift the axis and shift which sentences are reflected. We have not characterised this sensitivity.
- The composite coherence score has unproven predictive validity. Treat all numbers above as descriptive.
